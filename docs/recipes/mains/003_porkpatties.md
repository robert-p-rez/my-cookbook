# Lemongrass and Ginger Pork Patties

**Course:** Main  
**Cuisine:** Dairy free, gluten free, nut free, sugar free  
**Prep Time:** 20 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes  
**Total Time:** 40 minutes  
**Servings:** 5  
**Calories:** 323 kcal (per serving)

---

## Ingredients

- **500 g (18 oz)** lean pork mince  
- 1 egg  
- 1.5 tablespoons minced or finely chopped lemongrass  
- 1 tablespoon grated ginger  
  *fresh or jarred*  
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed  
  *or 2 teaspoons jarred minced garlic*  
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce  
  *use gluten-free soy sauce or tamari if needed*  
- 2 teaspoons honey or maple syrup  
- 3 spring onions, finely sliced  
- 1/2 cup quick oats or breadcrumbs  
  *use gluten-free oats/breadcrumbs if needed*  
- Pinch of black pepper  
- Extra virgin olive oil, for cooking  

---

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to **180°C (350°F)**. Line a large baking tray with baking paper. Set aside.

2. Prepare aromatics: peel and grate the ginger, crush the garlic, and finely slice the spring onions.

3. In a large bowl, combine everything **except** the olive oil: pork mince, egg, oats/breadcrumbs, lemongrass, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, honey, spring onions, and pepper.  
   Mix well and shape into **14–16 small patties** (like large, slightly flattened meatballs).

4. Heat a large frying pan over high heat and add 1 tablespoon olive oil.  
   Cook patties for **2 minutes per side**, just until browned.  
   Transfer browned patties to the lined baking tray as you go.

5. Once all patties are browned, place the baking tray in the oven and bake for **10–12 minutes**, or until cooked through.

6. Serve patties with rice or noodles and a crunchy fresh salad.  
   Leftovers will keep refrigerated for **up to 3 days**.

---

## Notes

- Nutrition values use regular pork mince; lean mince will alter the numbers.  
- You can substitute **chicken mince** or **turkey mince** for pork.  
- Patties can be frozen **raw or cooked**. Place baking paper between patties to prevent sticking.  
- **Dressing suggestion:**  
  Mix sweet chili sauce, a splash of soy sauce, and fresh lime juice.  
  Rice vinegar can be used instead of lime.  
  Add crushed peanuts or toasted cashews for extra crunch and flavor.
