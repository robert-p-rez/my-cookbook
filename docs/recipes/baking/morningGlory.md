# Simple Morning Glory Muffins

Moist, warmly spiced muffins packed with carrots, apple, raisins, and pecans.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups (260g) whole wheat flour (measured correctly)
- 2 teaspoons baking soda
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/3 cup (35g) ground flax (optional)
- 1/2 cup (64g) unsalted chopped pecans
- 3 large eggs
- 1/2 cup (100g) packed light or dark brown sugar
- 1/4 cup (85g) honey  
  - *Substitute:* agave or maple syrup, or use 1/4 cup additional brown sugar
- 1/3 cup (80ml) vegetable oil, canola oil, or melted coconut oil
- 1/3 cup (60g) smooth applesauce  
  - *Substitute:* 1/3 cup additional oil or mashed banana (may be slightly drier)
- 1 teaspoon orange zest
- 1/4 cup (60ml) orange juice or pineapple juice
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/2 cup (75g) raisins, soaked in warm water for 10 minutes and drained  
  - *Optional:* use golden raisins, dried cranberries, or omit
- 2 cups (260g) shredded carrots (about 4 large)
- 1 cup (140g) shredded or grated apple (about 1 apple; any variety works, e.g., Granny Smith)

## Directions

1. **Preheat oven** to 425°F (218°C). Spray a 12-count muffin pan with nonstick spray or line with cupcake liners. This recipe makes about 16 muffins, so prepare a second pan if needed.

2. **Mix dry ingredients:** In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, salt, flax (if using), and pecans. Set aside.

3. **Mix wet ingredients:** In a medium bowl, whisk together eggs, brown sugar, honey, oil, applesauce, orange zest, orange juice, and vanilla until combined.

4. **Combine:** Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients. Stir gently a few times, then fold in raisins, shredded carrots, and apple. Mix just until combined—do not overmix.

5. **Fill pans:** Spoon batter into liners, filling each all the way to the top.

6. **Bake:**  
   - Bake for 5 minutes at 425°F (218°C).  
   - Without removing muffins, reduce oven temperature to 350°F (177°C).  
   - Bake for an additional 18 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

7. **Cool:** Allow muffins to cool in the pan for several minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

## Notes

- The initial high temperature helps create tall muffin tops.
- Store at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate up to 1 week.
- Muffins freeze well for up to 3 months.
