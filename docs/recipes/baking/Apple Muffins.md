# Apple Muffins with Crumb Topping & Maple Syrup Icing

## Ingredients

### Muffins
- 1 and 3/4 cups all-purpose flour or whole wheat  
  *OR replace 3/4 cup with protein pancake mix*
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup softened unsalted butter  
  *or replace 1/4 cup of the butter with applesauce for a healthier version*
- 1/2 cup packed light or dark brown sugar
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs, at room temperature
- 1/2 cup Greek yogurt (unsweetened or vanilla), at room temperature
- 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
- 1/4 cup milk (any kind), at room temperature
- 1 and 1/2 cups peeled & chopped apples

### Crumb Topping
- 1/4 cup dark brown sugar  
  - 1/4 cup granulated sugar  
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/4 cup melted unsalted butter
- 2/3 cup all-purpose flour or whole wheat flour

### Maple Syrup Icing
- 1 cup confectioners’ sugar
- 3 tablespoons half and half
- 1 teaspoon maple syrup

---

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to **425°F**. Spray a muffin pan with nonstick spray or line with cupcake liners.  
   *Recipe makes 12–14 muffins.*

2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Set aside.

3. In a separate bowl, beat the butter and both sugars on high speed until smooth and creamy (about 2 minutes).  
   Add the eggs, yogurt, and vanilla. Beat on medium speed for 1 minute, then increase to high speed until the mixture is combined and mostly creamy.  
   *It’s ok if it looks slightly curdled.* Scrape down the bowl as needed.

4. With the mixer on low speed, add the dry ingredients and milk into the wet mixture. Mix until no flour pockets remain.  
   Fold in the chopped apples.

5. **Make the crumb topping:**  
   In a medium bowl, mix the brown sugar, granulated sugar, and cinnamon. Stir in the melted butter, then gently mix in the flour with a fork.  
   Keep the mixture as large crumbles and do not over-mix (overmixing turns it into a paste).

6. Spoon the batter evenly into muffin cups, filling each to the top. Add crumb topping to each and gently press so it sticks.

7. Bake for **5 minutes at 425°F**, then (without opening the oven) reduce temperature to **350°F**.  
   Bake an additional **15–18 minutes**, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.  
   Total bake time: **20–23 minutes**.

8. Allow muffins to cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

9. **Make the icing:**  
   Whisk together all icing ingredients. Drizzle over warm or cooled muffins.

10. Store iced or plain muffins at room temperature for **2–3 days**, or in the refrigerator for **up to 1 week**.
